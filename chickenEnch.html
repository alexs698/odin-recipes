Chicken Enchiladas

An Easy Recipe for Enchiladas Made From Scratch

The sweet, earthy, and mild flavor of the Ancho chile makes it the perfect base for an enchilada sauce.

Ingredients

    Chicken

  • 2 large chicken breasts
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 8-10 cups of water
  • 1/2 medium sized white onion
  • 10 whole black peppercorns

    • Sauce

  • 4 plum tomatoes
  • 1/2 medium sized white onion
  • 5 cloves fresh garlic
  • 5 ancho chiles
  • 1 tspn ground cumin
  • 1 tspn Mexican oregano
  • 1 tspn cocoa powder
  • 1/2 tspn ground Ceylon cinnamon
  • 1/2 tspn ground allspice
  • 2 tspn chicken stock base

    • Assembly

  • 24 corn tortillas
  • Any amount of cheese of choice

    Directions

    Chicken

  1. Bring 8-10 cups of water to a boil. Add salt, bay leaves, peppercorns, and half an onion to the pot.(Onion should be cut into two large quarter pieces, still attached to the root stem. Remove paper skin.)
  2. Reduce boil to a simmer, gently submerge chicken breasts. Simmer for 15-20 minutes until internal temperature reaches 165 degrees fahrenheit.
  3. Set aside one cup of chicken poaching water for the red sauce.
  4. Remove chicken breasts and onion. Put on a plate and let cool to room temperature. Using a fork or your hands, pull chicken apart into small pieces. Dice the cooked pieces of onion and combine with the pulled/shredded chicken. This is the filling for the enchiladas.
  5. Discard remaining water, bay leaves, and peppercorns.

    Enchilada Sauce

  1. Remove stems, seeds, and seed veins from Ancho chiles. In a dry cast-iron skillet, gently toast chiles to warm up their flavors. Be careful not to burn the chile peppers, as they can turn bitter in flavor.
  2. Bring 2 cups of water to boil. Place toasted chiles in a bowl, pour hot water over the chiles, cover with plastic wrap, and let steam for 15 - 20 minutes.
  3. While chiles are steaming, add tomatoes, garlic cloves, and the quartered onion to the skillet. (Cut onions the same way as for the poached chicken, peeled, quartered, attached to the root stem.)
  4. Gently toast and char these ingredients to cook them slightly and give them a roasted flavor. Rotate the ingredients fairly often, so they get even charring on all sides.
  5. Add these ingredients to a blender with the rehydrated Ancho chiles. Add 1 cup of the reserved chicken poaching liquid along with a 1/3 cup of the chile pepper broth. Pulse and blend these ingredients until thoroughly pureed.
  6. Strain sauce through a wire sieve into a cooking pot. Add spices and chicken broth base to the red sauce and stir to combine. Simmer and gently reduce sauce for 20 minutes. Taste sauce and adjust seasoning and salt amount to your preference.

    Assembly

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees and lightly warm the corn tortillas in a skillet, and keep them warm in a clean kitchen towel.
  2. In a casserole dish, pour enough red sauce to cover the bottom of the dish.
  3. Place shredded chicken on a warmed tortilla and roll into a log shape. Lay the stuffed tortilla into the dish, and repeat until the dish is as filled. (You may require 2 casserole pans for this full recipe.)
  4. Cover stuffed tortillas with more sauce on top, and coat everything in shredded or crumbled cheese.
  5. Bake in the oven for 15-20 minutes or until the cheese has melted.
  6. Enjoy.
